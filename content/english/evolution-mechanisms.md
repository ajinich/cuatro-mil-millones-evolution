# Evolution Mechanisms

## Core Definition

Evolution is defined as **"changes in the hereditary traits of populations over time"**, noting that **"life on Earth has evolved gradually over more than 3.9 billion years"**.

## Key Mechanisms

The site identifies ten major evolutionary processes:

1. **Variability within a species** - Natural differences among individuals
2. **Hereditability of traits** - Passage of characteristics to descendants
3. **Genetic mutations** - Changes in DNA
4. **Natural selection** - "individuals better suited for the environment...leave more descendants"
5. **Genetic drift** - Random changes in gene frequencies
6. **Gene flow** - Movement of genes between populations
7. **Sexual selection** - Mate choice driving trait evolution
8. **Adaptive radiation** - Rapid species diversification
9. **Speciation** - Formation of new species
10. **Geological time** - The vast timeframe enabling evolution

## Central Principle

**Natural selection is one of the principal mechanisms** although it is not the only one. Advantageous traits persist because they provide **"a reproductive advantage"** to individuals possessing them.

## The Process

Species undergo genetic changes that pass to descendants, branching into new and diverse species. Over many generations, species can evolve into something vastly different from their original form. This cumulative process has far-reaching consequences across geological time.
